Introduction

Amoxicillin is a widely prescribed penicillin-type antibiotic used to treat a range of bacterial infections, including bladder infections — also known as urinary tract infections (UTIs). It works by stopping the growth of bacteria, allowing your immune system to clear the infection.

If your doctor has prescribed amoxicillin for a bladder infection, understanding the correct dosage, how long to take it, and the possible side effects can help you recover faster and reduce the risk of the infection returning.

What Is the Standard Amoxicillin Dose for a Bladder Infection?

Adult Dosage

For most healthy adults, the standard amoxicillin dosage for an uncomplicated bladder infection falls into one of two ranges:

  • 500 mg every 8 hours (three times daily) for 5 to 7 days
  • 875 mg every 12 hours (twice daily) for 3 to 7 days

According to FDA-approved prescribing information, the 875 mg twice-daily regimen is often preferred for uncomplicated lower urinary tract infections in adults because it maintains a steadier level of the antibiotic in your bloodstream throughout the day and is easier to follow consistently. [1]

It is important to note that amoxicillin is not always the first-choice antibiotic for bladder infections. Medications such as trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole (Bactrim) or nitrofurantoin (Macrobid) are often preferred because some strains of E. coli—the bacteria responsible for most urinary tract infections—have developed resistance to amoxicillin over time. [2] Your doctor may prescribe amoxicillin if a urine culture shows that the bacteria causing your infection are sensitive to it.

Dosage for Elderly Patients

Older adults may require extra care when it comes to antibiotic dosing. Kidney function often declines with age, which means the body may clear amoxicillin more slowly. If this is not taken into account, the medication can build up to higher-than-intended levels in the body and increase the risk of side effects.

According to MedlinePlus, patients with impaired kidney function may need a reduced dose or longer intervals between doses to prevent the drug from building up in the body. [4] For elderly patients, doctors commonly start with the lower 500 mg dose, extend the dosing interval, and monitor kidney function throughout the course of treatment.

How Long Does Amoxicillin Take to Work for a UTI?

Most people begin feeling noticeably better within 24 to 48 hours of their first dose. The burning sensation during urination, the sense of urgency, and the lower abdominal discomfort typically ease within the first couple of days.

However, feeling better does not mean the infection is completely gone. Stopping amoxicillin early is one of the most common mistakes patients make. The Mayo Clinic advises that completing the full antibiotic course as prescribed is essential for preventing the infection from returning and for reducing the chance that antibiotic-resistant bacteria will develop. [3] Always finish every dose your doctor prescribed — even if you feel completely well by day three.

What If Amoxicillin Doesn't Work for My Bladder Infection?

If your symptoms have not improved after 48 to 72 hours, contact your doctor. There are several possible reasons why the antibiotic may not be providing relief.

Antibiotic Resistance

Antibiotic resistance is a serious and growing public health concern in the United States. According to the CDC, antibiotic-resistant infections affect more than 2.8 million Americans every year, and not completing a prescribed antibiotic course is one of the leading contributors to the problem. [5] This is exactly why urine cultures are so valuable — they confirm which specific antibiotics will actually eliminate the bacteria in your infection.

Possible Kidney Infection

If you develop fever, chills, back or flank pain, or nausea alongside your UTI symptoms, the infection may have moved from your bladder to your kidneys — a condition called pyelonephritis. Kidney infections are significantly more serious and often require a different antibiotic, a longer treatment course, or intravenous antibiotics in a hospital setting. [3] Never delay seeking care if you develop these additional symptoms.

Revisiting the Diagnosis

Sometimes what feels like a bladder infection has another cause. Conditions like interstitial cystitis, sexually transmitted infections, or kidney stones can produce very similar symptoms. If amoxicillin is not helping, your doctor may order additional tests to rule out other underlying causes.

Side Effects of Amoxicillin for UTI Treatment

Common Side Effects

Most people tolerate amoxicillin well, but some experience mild side effects — particularly in the first few days of treatment:

  • Nausea or stomach upset [1]
  • Diarrhea [1]
  • Headache
  • Skin rash — more likely in patients who also have a concurrent viral infection such as mononucleosis [1]

Taking amoxicillin with food can help ease stomach discomfort significantly.

Less Common Side Effects

  • Metallic taste in the mouth
  • Mild skin irritation or itching [4]
  • Vaginal yeast infection — antibiotics can disrupt the natural balance of bacteria and yeast in the body [4]

Rare but Serious Risks

⚠️ Severe Allergic Reaction: In rare cases, amoxicillin can cause a life-threatening allergic reaction known as anaphylaxis [1]. Symptoms include hives, swelling of the face or throat, and difficulty breathing. If any of these occur, stop the medication immediately and seek emergency care. The FDA notes that serious and occasionally fatal hypersensitivity reactions have been reported with penicillin-class antibiotics, the group to which amoxicillin belongs. [1] If you have a known penicillin allergy, do not take amoxicillin — inform your doctor so they can prescribe a safe alternative. [2]

⚠️ C. difficile-Associated Diarrhea: Antibiotics can disrupt the natural balance of gut bacteria, sometimes allowing Clostridioides difficile (C. diff) to overgrow. Symptoms include severe watery diarrhea, abdominal cramping, and fever. If these develop during or after your course of amoxicillin, contact your doctor right away. [4]

Tips to Reduce Side Effects

  • Always take amoxicillin with food to reduce stomach upset [4]
  • Stay well hydrated throughout your course of treatment
  • Do not skip doses — take them at evenly spaced intervals each day
  • Complete the full prescribed course even if symptoms improve early [3]
  • Contact your doctor before stopping the medication for any reason

When to Speak With a Doctor

Contact your healthcare provider if your symptoms do not begin to improve within 48 to 72 hours, if side effects become severe or persistent, or if you develop new symptoms like fever, chills, or back pain. Your doctor may adjust the dose, switch to a different antibiotic based on your urine culture results, or investigate whether the infection has spread.

Conclusion

Amoxicillin is a proven and effective antibiotic for bladder infections when the bacteria causing the infection are susceptible to it. The standard adult dose is either 500 mg three times daily or 875 mg twice daily for 3 to 7 days, depending on the severity of the infection and individual patient factors. [1] Always complete the full prescribed course, stay alert to warning signs that the infection may be spreading, and check in with your doctor if symptoms do not begin improving within a few days. Frequently Asked Questions

Can I take amoxicillin for a UTI without seeing a doctor?

In the United States, amoxicillin is a prescription-only medication. Taking leftover antibiotics or borrowing them from someone else is unsafe and can mask symptoms of a more serious condition. Always get a proper diagnosis and valid prescription first. [5]

Is 500 mg of amoxicillin enough for a bladder infection?

For mild, uncomplicated bladder infections where the bacteria are confirmed to be susceptible, 500 mg taken three times a day for 5 to 7 days is generally effective. Your doctor will choose the right dose based on your urine test results and overall health. [1]

Can amoxicillin treat a kidney infection too?

Amoxicillin can be used for mild kidney infections, but more severe or complicated cases typically require stronger antibiotics and a longer duration of treatment. If you suspect your infection has spread to your kidneys, see a doctor promptly. [3]

What should I do if I miss a dose?

Take the missed dose as soon as you remember. If it is almost time for your next scheduled dose, skip the missed one and continue as normal. Never take two doses at the same time to catch up. [4]

Can I drink alcohol while taking amoxicillin?

Alcohol does not directly interact with amoxicillin, but it can worsen side effects like nausea and dehydration, which may slow your recovery. It is best to limit or avoid alcohol until the infection has fully cleared.
